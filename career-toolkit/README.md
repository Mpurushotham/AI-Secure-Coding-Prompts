# Career Toolkit — AI Prompts for the Job Search

**A companion toolkit for engineers using AI to run a faster, sharper job search — oriented toward software, security, platform, and cloud roles.**

This is an *adjacent* collection to the secure-coding prompt library, not another security category. Same copy-paste model: paste a prompt into any AI assistant, give it your real material (job description, your background, the interview), and iterate.

---

## The one rule that matters: be truthful

AI makes it trivially easy to fabricate — a skill you don't have, a metric you invented, a project you didn't do. **Don't.** Every prompt here is built to *tailor, sharpen, and translate* your real experience to a specific role, never to invent it. Fabrication surfaces in interviews and reference checks and ends searches. The goal is to present your genuine story in the strongest, most relevant, honest form — and to save you the hours of blank-page friction that keep good candidates from applying.

Two supporting habits:
- **Keep a "brag doc"** — a running file of what you shipped, the impact, and the numbers. It's the raw material every prompt below turns into tailored output. Start it now; update it monthly.
- **You are the editor.** AI drafts; you verify every claim, cut the fluff, and make it sound like you. Recruiters can smell unedited AI text — use it to get to a strong draft fast, then own the final word.

## How to get the most out of these

1. **Feed real inputs.** Paste the actual job description and your actual brag-doc entries — vague inputs give generic output.
2. **Tailor per application.** A resume/letter tuned to one JD beats a generic one sent to fifty. These prompts make per-application tailoring fast enough to actually do.
3. **Verify every number and claim** the AI produces before it goes out.
4. **Stack them:** Job Search Strategy → Resume Tailoring → Cover Letter → (apply) → Follow-up → Interview Prep → STAR.
